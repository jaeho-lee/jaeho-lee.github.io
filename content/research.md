# research

I prove theorems and design algorithms for machine learning.

## goals

My long-term goal is to make AI more ***responsible*** --- accessible, sustainable, and righteous. I currently focus on ***efficient ai***. In particular, I aim to characterize the fundamental trade-offs among three factors:
- predictive performance
- inference cost
- training cost

While I prefer to keep my algorithms domain-agnostic, I enjoy working with data modalities where the efficiency is critical---e.g., robotics or surgical imaging.

## perspectives

I seek to gain a deeper understanding on the ***algorithmic biases*** of machine learning. Understanding the bias will unveil why certain trade-offs emerge, and harnessing it will provide us a crucial tool to achieve the Pareto optimal.

Here are some examples:
- **Initialization** ([a](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.00827),[b](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.04779)). We theoretically characterize how the training speed trades with generalizability, as we change the *initialization scale* of neural nets.
- **Attention sinks** ([c](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.12016), [d](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.04547)). We harness the attention-biasing phenomenon to develop a model pre-processing algorithm to improve its post-compression performance.  
- **Data transformations.** ([e](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.17094)) We discover that invertible transformations of the training data can dramatically affect both the training speed and the generalizability, and harness this phenomenon to accelerate neural field training.
